# Rubric: Paper to Replication

## What Good Execution Looks Like

- Paper logic is converted into concrete, runnable steps.
- Data requirements are explicit and testable.
- Replication scope is clearly bounded.
- Unknowns are surfaced early.

## Minimum Acceptable Outputs

- structured paper summary
- data requirements list
- replication plan
- implementation checklist
- risks/unknowns section

## Common Mistakes

- narrative summary with no operational plan
- no sample construction detail
- no variable transformation mapping
- no dependency tracking
- hidden assumptions about missing methods

## Review Checklist

- Is replication scope explicit?
- Are data requirements sufficient to start execution?
- Are implementation steps ordered and testable?
- Are unknowns and blockers visible?
- Is the plan consistent with the stated estimand?
