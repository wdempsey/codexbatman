# Rubric: Result Communication

## What Good Execution Looks Like

- Communication is concise, accurate, and audience-aligned.
- Claims are tightly linked to evidence.
- Uncertainty and caveats are explicit.
- Recommendations are concrete and decision-relevant.

## Minimum Acceptable Outputs

- clear finding summary
- audience-facing interpretation
- uncertainty/caveats section
- recommendation/next steps
- at least one requested format artifact

## Common Mistakes

- certainty inflation
- missing caveats
- no action recommendation
- mismatched tone for audience
- technical detail without decision interpretation

## Review Checklist

- Are claims supported by the underlying artifacts?
- Is uncertainty clearly communicated?
- Are caveats specific and actionable?
- Is the recommendation decision-ready?
- Is output format appropriate for audience?
