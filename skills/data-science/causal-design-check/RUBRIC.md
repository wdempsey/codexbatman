# Rubric: Causal Design Check

## What Good Execution Looks Like

- Causal question is translated to a clear estimand.
- Assumptions are explicit and design-specific.
- Threats, overlap, and interference are analyzed.
- Interpretation limits are precise and conservative.

## Minimum Acceptable Outputs

- estimand definition
- identification assumptions
- threat inventory
- overlap/interference assessment plan
- proceed/halt recommendation

## Common Mistakes

- no estimand
- generic assumptions not tied to design
- no overlap evaluation
- no interference analysis
- overconfident interpretation language

## Review Checklist

- Is estimand explicitly and correctly defined?
- Are assumptions linked to identification strategy?
- Are major threats and mitigations documented?
- Are overlap and interference addressed?
- Are interpretation limits clear for decision-makers?
